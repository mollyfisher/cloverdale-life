The Five Funding Asks, Side by Side

A plain-language comparison of what each community partner is asking for in the FY 26–27 budget — and what each ask buys.

April 28, 2026

Five community organizations submitted funding requests for the FY 26–27 budget cycle: the Senior Center, the Historical Society, the Chamber of Commerce, the Performing Arts Center, and the resilience program known as CloveReady. The dollar amounts vary, the cases vary, and the relationship of each request to the City's general fund varies more than the workshop deck made clear.

This piece lines up the five asks against four questions: How much. For what. From which fund. What does the City get back.

The shape of the requests

Total ask across the five: roughly $700,000, on a draft General Fund expense base of about $11.4M. Three of the five requests are renewals of ongoing arrangements; two are new asks or substantial expansions. The Senior Center request is the largest single line; CloveReady is the smallest but arguably the most novel in scope.
