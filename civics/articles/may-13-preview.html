May 13 City Council Meeting: What's in the Packet

A short agenda of mostly procedural items, followed by what is likely to come up in public comment.

May 8, 2026

The Cloverdale City Council meets Wednesday, May 13 at 6:00 p.m. at the Cloverdale Performing Arts Center, 209 N. Cloverdale Boulevard. The meeting will also be streamed on Zoom (Meeting ID 823 1668 4527) and YouTube at cloverdale.net/youtube. Written public comment may be submitted to publiccomments@ci.cloverdale.ca.us.

The agenda is short. The substantive items in the packet are the following:

Recognitions

  • Proclamation — Lyme and Tick-Borne Diseases Awareness Month. Recipient listed as TBD.
  • Proclamation — Older Americans Month. Recipient: Cloverdale Kiwanis Club.
  • Proclamation — National Municipal Clerks Week. Recipient: City Clerk and Public Information Officer Mike Maloney.

Presentation

Lisa G. Carreño will present an overview of 211 Sonoma County, the United Way-affiliated information and referral service. Per the agenda, 211 Sonoma County responds to more than 17,000 callers per year with information on shelter, healthcare, food, utilities, mental health, legal services, and other community programs.   • F.1 — Approval of April 22, 2026 draft minutes
  • F.2 — Approval of May 5, 2026 draft minutes (the budget workshop)
  • F.3 — Amendment to the Cloverdale Citizen Recognition Program (CCRP). What's changing →

New Business

  • H.1 — Receive Report on Code Enforcement Services. Action: receive and file. What the report says →

Not on the Agenda, But Likely in Public Comment

The Public Comments file as of May 6 contains written communications on several topics not appearing on Wednesday's agenda, including the proposed Esmeralda development, the proposed water and wastewater rate increases, and a paving request for Mayor Way.
