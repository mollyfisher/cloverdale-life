Council Preview

A Light Agenda for May 13 — But Don't Mistake That for a Quiet Room

Three proclamations, a 211 presentation, a tweak to the recognition program, and a code enforcement update. The packet is short. The comment period probably won't be.

May 8, 2026

The Cloverdale City Council meets this Wednesday, May 13, at 6 p.m. at the Performing Arts Center, 209 N. Cloverdale Boulevard. You can show up in person, watch on Zoom (Meeting ID 823 1668 4527) or YouTube (cloverdale.net/youtube), or send written comment to publiccomments@ci.cloverdale.ca.us. Verbal comment is in person only — three minutes per speaker.

What follows is a quick map of what's actually in the packet, what it means, and a heads-up about what will likely come up that isn't in the packet. Each topic links to a fuller breakdown.

The Recognitions

Three proclamations open the meeting. Lyme Disease Awareness Month — recipient TBD. Lyme is a tick-borne bacterial infection, and Sonoma County has documented Lyme-positive ticks in the western part of the county, so this isn't an abstract concern for anyone who walks dogs in tall grass. Older Americans Month, accepted by the Cloverdale Kiwanis Club. National Municipal Clerks Week, accepted by City Clerk Mike Maloney. Worth noting: Cloverdale's clerk function carries the same legal scope as larger Sonoma County cities with the lowest staffing of any of them.

211 Sonoma County

Lisa Carreño of United Way will present on 211 — the social-services analog to 911. Free, confidential, multilingual, 24/7. Last year it fielded more than 17,000 calls from Sonoma County. Why this is worth twenty minutes of your attention →

Cloverdale has no Code Enforcement Department. Wednesday's report outlines three modest steps to professionalize a function the city has been quietly holding together with one Associate Planner and a job description that doesn't formally include the work. The full breakdown →
