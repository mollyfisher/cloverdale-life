211 Sonoma County Presentation Scheduled for May 13 Council Meeting

United Way's Lisa G. Carreño will present an overview of 211, the information and referral service that connects callers to health and human service resources.

May 8, 2026

Item E.1 on Wednesday's agenda is a presentation by Lisa G. Carreño on 211 Sonoma County. Per the agenda description:

"The presentation provides an overview of 211 Sonoma County and the services it provides to connect residents with health and human service resources. Each year, 211 Sonoma County responds to more than 17,000 callers throughout Sonoma County, providing information and resources related to shelter, healthcare, food, utilities, mental health, legal services, consumer assistance, and other community support programs."

What 211 Is

211 is a three-digit phone number designated by the Federal Communications Commission for community information and referral services, parallel to 911 for emergencies and 311 for non-emergency municipal services. Calls to 211 are routed to local providers; in Sonoma County, the service is operated by United Way of the Wine Country. The service is free, confidential, and multilingual.

Per 211 Sonoma County's published materials, the categories of assistance include:

  • Shelter and housing
  • Healthcare access
  • Food assistance
  • Utility assistance
  • Mental health support
  • Legal services
  • Consumer assistance
  • Other community support programs

During declared emergencies — wildfires, floods, public safety power shutoffs — 211 also serves as a non-emergency information line for evacuation orders, shelter locations, and recovery resources.

How to Reach 211

  • Phone: Dial 2-1-1
  • Online: 211sonoma.org
  • Hours: 24 hours, seven days a week
  • Languages: Multilingual access available

What Action Is Being Asked For Wednesday

The agenda lists the item as a presentation. No Council action is requested.
