A Phone Number Worth Knowing Before You Need It: 211 Sonoma County

211 is the social-services analog to 911 — free, confidential, multilingual, 24/7. Wednesday's Council presentation is a short version of why every household should keep the number in mind.

May 8, 2026

If you've never used 211, the elevator pitch is short: 211 is what 911 would be if 911 handled food, housing, mental health, legal aid, utility assistance, and disaster information instead of emergencies. It's free, confidential, multilingual, and runs 24 hours a day. You dial three digits.

Lisa Carreño of United Way will present an overview of 211 Sonoma County to the City Council on Wednesday, May 13. The number worth keeping in mind from the presentation: last year 211 Sonoma County fielded more than 17,000 calls — connecting residents to housing, food, mental health support, healthcare, legal services, consumer protection, and other community programs.

What 211 Actually Does

The most useful way to think about 211 is as a directory and a navigation service. The directory is large — thousands of programs, agencies, and nonprofits — and changes constantly. Even people who work in social services have trouble keeping track. 211 staff do that as their job, and they take calls from anyone in Sonoma County.

The kinds of calls they handle include:

  • Need help paying rent or finding housing
  • Looking for food assistance, food banks, or CalFresh enrollment
  • Mental health support — including non-crisis support and warm handoffs to specialists
  • Healthcare access, including help finding a clinic or navigating Medi-Cal
  • Legal aid — landlord-tenant issues, immigration, consumer protection
  • Senior services and caregiver resources
  • Utility assistance and bill help
  • Disaster information during fires, floods, and PSPS events

Why It Comes Up at City Council

211 doesn't typically need a Council vote — it's a county-wide service that runs whether or not Cloverdale endorses it. The presentation is informational, but it lands at a useful moment. Cloverdale residents tend to learn about resources like this during a crisis, not before. A short presentation in a public meeting, followed by mention in city communications and a few neighbors telling other neighbors, is one of the cheapest civic interventions a town can run.

The Mechanics

  • Phone: Dial 2-1-1
  • Online: 211sonoma.org
  • Hours: 24 hours, every day
  • Cost: Free
  • Languages: Multilingual

If you have an elderly parent, a neighbor going through a hard stretch, or just a piece of paper on the fridge for the small emergencies that aren't really emergencies — this is the number that belongs there.

Sources: City of Cloverdale Agenda for May 13, 2026, Item E.1; 211 Sonoma County (211sonoma.org).
