What's in the Public Comment File for May 13

Topics in the written public comment file as of May 6, 2026, including correspondence on the Esmeralda development, Proposition 218 protests of the proposed water and sewer rate increases, and a paving request for Mayor Way.

May 8, 2026

The Public Comments file posted with the May 13 agenda contains written communications received by the City through May 6, 2026. The file includes correspondence on topics that are not on Wednesday's agenda. Under the Brown Act (California Government Code §54954.2), the Council cannot deliberate or take action on items not listed on the agenda. Speakers may still address the Council during the verbal public comment period, with three minutes typically allotted to each speaker.

The following is a summary of the topics that appear in the written file, with the source documents identified.

Esmeralda Development — Correspondence in the File

The Esmeralda project is a proposed multi-phase residential and mixed-use development on the former Masonite property. Per a letter in the file from the Dry Creek Rancheria Band of Pomo Indians dated April 24, 2026, the project as currently proposed includes "600+ homes, in a mix of apartments, townhomes, and single-family houses... a resort hotel, 200 units of senior living, two restaurants, office and commercial space, light-industrial space, a racquet club, two indoor pavilions, and possibly an elementary school."

Letter from the Dry Creek Rancheria Band of Pomo Indians

Tribal Chairman Chris Wright wrote to City Clerk Mike Maloney on April 24, 2026, expressing concerns about the project's reliance on a 2009 Environmental Impact Report (with addenda from 2016, 2018, and 2026) prepared for the earlier Alexander Valley Resort proposal. The letter states:

"The impacts to water, air, and land are significant to the Tribe as it is located in ancestral territory and upstream from the Rancheria... The Water Supply Assessment/Water Verification assumed 1,525 residents, but that does not include the increased traffic from resort guests, visitors, shop customers, workers in the commercial space, and deliveries."

The letter raises concerns about the project's location adjacent to the Russian River, the property's Superfund history, and pentachlorophenol-contaminated groundwater on the former Masonite property. The Tribe requests preparation of a new Environmental Impact Report.

Letter from Rollin C. Reineck, Jr.

Reineck, identified in his letter and attached resume as Director and Principal Scientist of Affiliated Researchers LLC and a Certified Professional Wetland Scientist with over 45 years of experience, wrote to Vice Mayor Todd Lands on May 3, 2026 and to Mayor Brian Wheeler on May 6, 2026. The letters identify what Reineck describes as omissions in the 2009 Final EIR and 2018 Addendum regarding threatened and endangered salmonid species in the Russian River, including coho salmon (which Reineck notes is listed as Endangered, not Threatened as stated in the 2009 EIR), steelhead, and chinook salmon.

Reineck identifies four areas he describes as lacking adequate analysis: impacts of project water demand on river flow regime; volumes of groundwater available below the riverbed during normal and drought years; river temperatures and their interaction with reduced flow; and water depths as they relate to salmonid rearing habitat and migration.

Petition by Jennifer Sullivan

Sullivan, a Cloverdale resident, has filed Petition No. A-2933 with the State Water Resources Control Board requesting a stay and review of the No Further Action determination issued for the former Masonite Wood Treatment Facility (Case No. 1NSO266) by the North Coast Regional Water Quality Control Board on January 21, 2026. A follow-up letter dated April 27, 2026 was forwarded to the Council for the public record.

The petition documents, per Sullivan's account of the administrative record, a rebound in pentachlorophenol concentrations at monitoring well MW-37 to approximately 130 µg/L in July 2024 (against a cleanup goal of 1 µg/L). The petition states that monitoring wells MW-37 and MW-12 — the final two monitoring wells at the site — were destroyed in December 2025, prior to issuance of the No Further Action determination. Sullivan requests that the State Board grant a stay of the NFA and review whether the closure decision complied with State Water Board Resolution 92-49.

Letters in support of the project

The file also contains letters in support of the project. Susan Nurse, a Cloverdale resident, sent a letter to the Press Democrat dated May 3, 2026, copied to the Council, stating: "Cloverdale's General Plan calls for 12,500 people by 2025 — well above the current population of 8,800 — along with water to support that growth. When this land is developed by Esmeralda, or anyone else, the land and the water is already planned for and available." The letter argues for "balanced fiscal growth" supporting middle and higher housing along with hotel transient occupancy taxes.

John Speicher, identified as a School Street homeowner, submitted notes from his April 22 verbal presentation to the Council in support of the project, citing his participation in Edge Esmeralda events and characterizing the project's organizing group as oriented toward "community connection."

Letter in opposition without a request for new EIR

Karen Harrison, identified in her April 22 email as a Cloverdale resident and former 30-year business owner, wrote: "Until we know what our water security is, I am opposed to more development. There is also the issue of lingering land and water contamination. Do an EIR!!!"

Proposition 218 Protests of Proposed Water and Sewer Rate Increases

The Council heard a presentation on the five-year water and wastewater rate study by Mark Hildebrand of Hildebrand Consulting at the April 22 meeting. The proposed rates are subject to the protest process established by Proposition 218 (California Constitution Article XIII D).

The public comment file contains formal written protests submitted under that process from the following individuals, all identified as City of Cloverdale property owners and/or ratepayers:

  • Amanda and Jarom Harrington (received May 4, 2026)
  • Mary Jo Winter, 420 Josephine Drive (dated April 29, 2026)
  • Debbie Young, 411 N. Washington Street (received May 4, 2026)
  • Claudia Clow (forwarded by Vice Mayor Lands May 1, 2026)

The protest letters cite affordability, what the writers describe as insufficient justification of proportionality under Proposition 218, economic impact on the community, and request that the City "explore cost-saving measures, budget adjustments, grant opportunities, or phased increases rather than implementing a significant immediate rate hike."

Mayor Way Paving Request

Diana Northness, identified as a resident of 100 Mayor Way, submitted a request to Public Works and the City Council on April 25, 2026, asking for inspection and prompt repaving of Mayor Way. The letter states the street experienced "accelerated and unusual wear due to the city bus mistakenly routed through it for several years," describes a previous patch repair as ineffective, and notes standing water and pedestrian safety concerns. The letter references the recent approval of Measure DD funds and asks for information on the timeline.

Other Correspondence in the File

The file also contains an email exchange between resident Angela Cordova and City Clerk Mike Maloney dated April 23–30, 2026, regarding the City's social media communications and questions about the Esmeralda project. Maloney's responses describe the City's social media accounts as managed as part of the City Clerk and Public Information Officer role, and state that an April 22 City social media post was developed by City staff independently.

How to Submit Public Comment

  • Verbal comment: in person at the May 13 meeting, three minutes per speaker.
  • Written comment: publiccomments@ci.cloverdale.ca.us. Submissions are public records under Government Code §54957.5.
