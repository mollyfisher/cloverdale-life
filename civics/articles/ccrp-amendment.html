Recognition Program

Council to Consider Removing the Two-Month Rotation From the Citizen Recognition Program

Item F.3 on the May 13 consent calendar would eliminate the rotating block structure adopted in September 2025. Each Councilmember would still be allowed one honoree per calendar year.

May 8, 2026

The Council adopted the Cloverdale Citizen Recognition Program (CCRP) by majority vote on September 24, 2025. Councilmember month assignments under the program were approved by motion on December 10, 2025. Item F.3 on Wednesday's consent calendar would amend the program.

What the Current Program Does

Per the program text approved in September 2025, the CCRP provides each Councilmember "the opportunity to publicly recognize residents, organizations, or groups whose contributions enhance civic spirit, improve quality of life, and positively reflect Cloverdale's values."

The current structure assigns each Councilmember a two-month block (Jan–Feb, Mar–Apr, May–Jun, Jul–Aug, Sep–Oct), with November and December excluded "due to frequent holiday meeting cancellations." Each Councilmember may recognize one honoree per calendar year, and no more than one honoree may be presented at any single Council meeting. Participation is optional. Members may trade blocks by mutual agreement; if a member does not recognize an honoree during their assigned block, the opportunity lapses for that year unless traded.

Honorees must live in or serve the Cloverdale community. The City reimburses up to $100 per recognition for gifts or gift cards, charged to the Council's operating supplies budget; amounts above $100 are the personal responsibility of the Councilmember.

What Is Being Proposed

According to the staff agenda report prepared by City Clerk Mike Maloney, the proposed amendment would "eliminate the rotating two-month assignment structure and instead allow each Councilmember the opportunity to recognize one honoree per calendar year." The limit of no more than one recognition per Council meeting would be retained.

The staff report cites the following reasons for the amendment:

"During implementation of the program, staff and Councilmembers identified logistical challenges associated with the rotating assignment structure. Council meeting cancellations, honoree availability, and coordination within limited recognition timeframes created scheduling and administrative difficulties for both staff and Councilmembers."

Financial and Procedural Notes

The agenda report lists no financial impact for the amendment and indicates the action is not a project under CEQA. The recommended action is to amend the program by motion. The item is on the consent calendar, meaning it would be approved together with other consent items in a single vote unless pulled for separate discussion by a Councilmember or member of the public.
