A Calendar Gets Less Calendar-y: The Recognition Program Tune-Up

Eight months after adopting a structured program for honoring residents, the Council is dropping the rotation that turned out to be more origami than calendar.

May 8, 2026

Last September the City Council adopted the Cloverdale Citizen Recognition Program — a structured way for Councilmembers to honor residents, families, organizations, and groups whose contributions make the town measurably better. The framework was straightforward: each Councilmember got a two-month block on the calendar (Jan–Feb, Mar–Apr, and so on, skipping November and December for holiday meeting cancellations). Each member could recognize one honoree per year. Block assignments were locked in last December.

The program worked the way most calendars work when they meet real life: imperfectly. Meetings get cancelled. Honorees have day jobs and travel plans. Trades between Councilmembers got tangled. Eight months in, staff and Councilmembers concluded the structure was producing more administrative friction than recognitions.

What's Changing

Wednesday's amendment removes the two-month windows entirely. Three things stay:

  • Each Councilmember can still recognize one honoree per calendar year.
  • No more than one recognition happens at any single Council meeting.
  • The same eligibility criteria — community impact, sustainability of contribution, alignment with Cloverdale's values — and the same $100 reimbursement cap on recognition gifts, charged to the Council's operating supplies budget.

What goes away is the rotation. Members can schedule a recognition when scheduling actually works for the honoree, the Council, and staff — instead of compressing a thank-you into a calendar window the honoree can't make.

Why It Matters (a Little)

This is a small change to a small program. But small programs are also where good intentions tend to die in administrative friction, and friction is what got reduced. If you've been meaning to nominate a neighbor, an organization, or a quietly indispensable volunteer, this is the version of the program that's likelier to actually get them in front of the Council in a reasonable timeframe.

Recognitions remain ceremonial — they aren't endorsements of political, religious, or other activity. They're a Certificate of Appreciation, a moment at a microphone, and a photo on the city's social media. Modest, but the kind of modest that adds up over years.

The Action

The Council is asked to amend the program by motion. No fiscal impact. No CEQA review needed. Item F.3 on the consent calendar.

Sources: City of Cloverdale Agenda Report for Item F.3, "Consider Amending the Cloverdale Citizen Recognition Program (CCRP)," May 13, 2026, prepared by City Clerk Mike Maloney; Attachment 1 — CCRP Redline Proposal. Original program adopted September 24, 2025; Councilmember month assignments approved December 10, 2025.
