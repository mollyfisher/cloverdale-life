About The Reader

The Cloverdale Reader is a long-form analytical publication covering the public record of the City of Cloverdale, California. Each piece takes a single question — a budget line, an ordinance, a contract, a decision — and works through it with care, in plain language, with the source materials cited so a reader can audit the work.

The Reader does not break news. It does not cover crime blotters, sports, or human-interest features. It does not carry advertising. It is a deliberately narrow publication, and the narrowness is the point.

Who writes it

The Reader is published by a Cloverdale resident with a working relationship with the Cloverdale Reveille, the city's weekly newspaper of record since 1879, on other civic projects. The Reader is not affiliated with the Reveille, the City of Cloverdale, or any other organization. Any errors are the writer's, not the city's.

Cadence and corrections

New analyses are published as the public record warrants — typically following City Council meetings, budget workshops, and major commission actions. Corrections are appended visibly to the affected article and noted on the homepage for thirty days.
