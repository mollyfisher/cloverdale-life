Methods

This page describes how The Cloverdale Reader handles sourcing, attribution, and the small editorial choices that go into a piece of analysis. Posting it permanently lets readers audit not just the conclusions but the way the conclusions are reached.

Sources

Every Reader article relies on primary documents: meeting agendas, presentation decks, executive summaries, ordinances, contracts, audit reports, and other materials posted to the City of Cloverdale's agenda center or otherwise part of the public record. Each article ends with a sourcing note that lists the documents it draws on and identifies the specific page or slide where contested figures came from.

Attribution

The Reader paraphrases. Direct quotation is reserved for cases where the speaker's exact wording is itself the news — typically because the wording was deliberate, sworn, or contractually binding. Statements made in public meetings are attributed to the person who made them, by name and role.

Numbers

Figures are reported as they appear in the source document, with one exception: where a budgeted total combines categories that an auditor would separate (for example, treating an interfund transfer as revenue), the Reader notes the convention and offers the alternative reading. The goal is to let a reader see the same number two ways and decide for themselves.

What the Reader is not

The Reader is not investigative journalism. It does not run sting operations, develop confidential sources, or report on private conduct. It works strictly from the public record. This is a deliberate choice, not a limitation: the public record is large, dense, and routinely under-read, and a publication that does nothing more than read it carefully is providing real value.

Corrections

Corrections are appended to the affected article in a visible block at the top, dated, with a brief description of what was wrong and what the correct version says. Corrections are also listed on the homepage for thirty days. The Reader does not silently edit published pieces.
